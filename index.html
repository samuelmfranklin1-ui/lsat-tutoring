Why It Matters

The most important
number in your application.

Law school admissions has never been more competitive. Applicant volume is up over 50% in two years, LSAT medians are rising at schools across every tier, and the difference between a good score and a great one can mean six figures in scholarship money — or the difference between your dream school and your safety.

+50%
Applicant Volume
Law school applicants are up more than 50% compared to the 2023–24 cycle and the current cycle is tracking even higher. The pool is larger and stronger than it's been in a generation — a score that felt competitive two years ago may no longer clear the bar.
+1.79 pts
Rising Medians
The average LSAT median across all ABA-accredited schools rose 1.79 points in a single admissions cycle, with only three schools seeing a decrease. Over the past decade, the average increase is 5 full points. The target keeps moving — and upward.
$82k/yr
Cost of Law School
Average law school cost of attendance now exceeds $82,000 per year. Students who score above a school's 75th percentile LSAT regularly receive half- or full-tuition merit scholarships. A meaningful score improvement can be worth $100,000 or more over three years.

Process

How it works.

01
Diagnostic Session

We start with a full timed practice test and a detailed review. You'll leave with a clear picture of your strengths, your weaknesses, and a roadmap to your target score.

02
Targeted Sessions

Each session is built around your specific gaps, not a generic curriculum. We drill the question types that cost you the most points — LR, RC, or both, and study the strategies I developed to get to the 99th percentile.

03
Track Progress

After every session I send a brief summary: what we covered, what to practice before next time, and our goals going forward. I'll work with you every step, up until test-day.

04
Admissions Consulting

Once your score is where it needs to be, I can help you navigate the application itself — school selection, personal statement strategy, and how your numbers will play across different tiers. Drawing on my own experience going through the process to a T-3 admit.

FAQ

Common questions.

What does the current LSAT cover?
As of 2024, the LSAT consists of Logical Reasoning and Reading Comprehension — Logic Games have been permanently removed. My curriculum is fully up to date with the current format and incorporates the strategies I learned and developed during my LSAT journey.
Where do we meet?
Your choice. In-person sessions take place at quiet libraries or cafés across Manhattan and Brooklyn. Remote sessions are via Google Meet. Both work equally well — you choose at booking.
How many sessions will I need?
Most students I work with do 15–30 sessions over 6–10 weeks. That said, every student is different. After your diagnostic session we'll map out a realistic, specific plan based on your target score and timeline.
What's your cancellation policy?
24 hours' notice is required to reschedule or cancel without charge. Cancellations under 24 hours forfeit the session unless we can find an alternative time that week.
Who is this tutoring best for?
I work well with students across the score range — from first-time test-takers building foundations to retakers chasing a specific target. If you're serious about the process and willing to put in work between sessions, we'll get along well.
